CyberPoetic zine!, Authorship in the age of AI

CyberPoetic zine! is a poetic web based zine project that explores the evolving relationship between poetry and technology. The first issue is called re_authorship. Through a blend of personal experiments and conversations with poets and artist, re_authorship looks into how AI is reshaping poetic and artistic expression. It's a glimpse at the blurred line between human and machine creativity, reflecting on the cultural and ethical implications of algorithmic language generation. By questioning authorship, ownership, and labour, CyberPoetic zine, with its first issue re_authorship, imagines new futures for poetry in the age of AI. The zine is also made using Pre Post Print tools (Weasyprint, Paged.js, html2print).

You can find its website here
cyberpoeticzine.net

Pen Plotting Party Workshops

Pen Plotting Party is a Rotterdam-based collective I’m part of, forged in the dynamic environment of Experimental Publishing at the Piet Zwart Institute. With backgrounds in graphic design, computing science, bookmaking, and mathematic, we bonded over pen plotters, the home office printer's predecessor lost to history. This 'obsolete' technology embodies a dynamic between new and old media, between rapid consumption and slow-paced, seamful performance. We have hosted a(n ongoing) series of events, building community around the multiplicity of methods pen plotters invite.

Costellazione Parallela, 20th century Italian poetesses

Costellazione Parallela, edited by Isabella Leardini and published by Vallecchi Editore, brings to light the voices of 20th-century Italian poetesses who were long overlooked, marginalised, or excluded from the literary canon. This book revives these influential yet forgotten figures, revealing how their voices have shaped, inspired, and reflected the inner lives of women of the 20th century and beyond. For this project I collaborated with some of my classmates to create the illustrations.
